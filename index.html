About Nepal

Land of Himalayas, culture, and natural beauty

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is mainly situated in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain.

With an estimated population of 30 million, Nepal is the 48th largest country by population and 93rd largest country by area. It borders China in the north and India in the south, east, and west.

Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth.

The country is multi-ethnic and multi-lingual, with Nepali as the official language. The capital and largest city is Kathmandu.

Top Attractions

Must-visit places in Nepal

Mount Everest

The world's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas.

Pashupatinath Temple

A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu.

Pokhara

Known as the "Tourist Capital of Nepal", Pokhara is famous for its tranquil atmosphere and beautiful scenery.

Madhyabindu-Aarungkhola

Your beautiful hometown in Nepal

Madhyabindu is a growing municipality in Nawalpur District of Nepal. Located in the beautiful hills of the Gandaki Province, it offers stunning views of the Himalayas and a peaceful environment.

Aarungkhola is a scenic area within Madhyabindu, known for its natural beauty and cultural significance. The region is surrounded by lush green forests, flowing rivers, and terraced fields.

The area is predominantly agricultural, with farming being the main occupation of the local people. The community is known for its warmth and hospitality towards visitors.

Madhyabindu-Aarungkhola offers opportunities for hiking, nature walks, and cultural experiences. The local festivals and traditions provide a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Nepal.

Nepalese Culture

Rich traditions and heritage

Religion

Nepal is a secular country with Hinduism and Buddhism as the main religions coexisting harmoniously.

Cuisine

Nepalese cuisine includes dal bhat (lentil soup with rice), momo (dumplings), and various ethnic foods.

Festivals

Major festivals include Dashain, Tihar, Holi, and Buddha Jayanti, celebrated with great enthusiasm.

Handicrafts

Nepal is famous for handicrafts like thangka paintings, wood carvings, and handmade paper products.
